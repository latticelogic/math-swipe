## Full description
Math Challenge is a mental-math game built around one idea: the tricks are
the fun part.

Swipe to answer. Every problem is multiple choice — swipe toward the right
answer and keep your streak alive. The difficulty adapts to you: quick,
confident answers push you up a level; a rough patch brings it gently back
down.

Learn why, not just how. The Magic Tricks library teaches 36 mental-math
shortcuts, and every lesson starts with the reason the trick works — the
algebra, the picture, or the story behind it. Then you practice until it's
yours.

35 topics — addition, subtraction, multiplication and division to start, then
times tables, powers, primes, fractions, percentages and negative numbers.
Eight hand-drawn teachers with their own voices keep you company. A daily
challenge gives everyone the same puzzle set each day, and the league shows
you how you stack up.

No ads. No subscriptions. The core game is free to try for 7 days, and one
small one-time purchase unlocks everything for good.

Made for ages 8 and up — and for the adults who secretly race their kids.

## ASO notes (owner decisions — not applied unilaterally)
- **Title keyword suffix.** Play allows a 30-char title; ours is "Math
  Challenge" (14). Appending a keyword — e.g. "Math Challenge: Mental Math"
  (27) — is a standard ASO lift because the title is the single most heavily
  weighted field. Held back as a brand call: the clean name may be worth more
  than the keyword. Owner's choice.
- **Operation keywords.** The full description now names addition, subtraction,
  multiplication and division explicitly — these are the highest-volume parent
  queries and were previously only implied. Play indexes the full description,
  so literal terms matter (accurate: the app has all four topics).
- **Short description** leads with "Mental math" (indexed) and stays 79/80.
  Kept warm over keyword-stuffed per the tone bar.
- **When any of these change, paste the edited field into Play Console →
  Grow → Store listing** — this file is the source of truth, the Console is a
  copy.
