Moving (additional channel, while keeping the web app)Math Challenge (formerly Math Swipe) from a web domain to Google Play—along with securing the Teacher Approved badge and getting into Play Pass—is a highly achievable pipeline for a Progressive Web App (PWA).Since your app is built on web technologies (React/Canvas API) and hosted on a registered domain, you will be deploying it using a Trusted Web Activity (TWA). This wraps your PWA in a native Android shell without needing to rewrite any code.Here is the exact roadmap to get this setup completed.1. Package the PWA via Trusted Web Activity (TWA)To get your web app into an Android App Bundle (.aab) for the Play Store, use Google's CLI tool, Bubblewrap, or the web-based GUI equivalent, PWABuilder.Step A: Configure Your Web App ManifestEnsure your hosted PWA at your registered domain has a rock-solid manifest.json. It must have:standalone or fullscreen display mode.A valid Service Worker with a functional fetch handler (for offline capability).High-res icons (specifically a 512x512 maskable icon).Step B: Build the PackageIf you prefer the command line, install and run Bubblewrap:Bashnpm install -g @bubblewrap/cli
bubblewrap init --manifest="https://mathchallenge.app/manifest.json"
bubblewrap build
This generates your signing keys and outputs a signed production digital asset bundle (app-release-signed.aab) ready for Google Play.Step C: Establish Digital Asset Links (Crucial)To prevent your app from showing a clunky browser URL bar at the top, you must prove you own the domain. Bubblewrap will give you a SHA-256 fingerprint. You must host a JSON file at your domain's root:[https://mathchallenge.app/.well-known/assetlinks.json](https://mathchallenge.app/.well-known/assetlinks.json)It will look like this:JSON[{
  "relation": ["delegate_permission/common.handle_all_urls"],
  "target": {
    "namespace": "android_app",
    "package_name": "your.package.name",
    "sha256_cert_fingerprints": ["YOUR_APP_SHA256_FINGERPRINT_HERE"]
  }
}]
2. Publish to the Google Play ConsoleTo launch on Google Play, follow these administrative steps:1.Register an Organization Account:$25 USD One-time fee.Set up a Google Play Developer Account. Choose an Organization Account rather than a Personal account. Personal accounts created recently face a grueling requirement where you must recruit 20 independent testers to run your app for 14 days straight before you can launch. Organization accounts bypass this testing gate. You will need your company's D-U-N-S number, corporate email, and phone number to verify identity.2.Complete the Families Policy Declarations:In the Play Console, navigate to Policy and Programs -> App Content. Because your target demographic includes children (e.g., Ages 5-7, as seen in your UI), you must check the boxes for these age groups. This binds you to Google's strict Families Policies.3.Ensure COPPA & GDPR Compliance:Privacy mandates.Your app must fully comply with COPPA (US) and GDPR (EU). Since it is a children's math app, you cannot collect sensitive data, use non-approved tracking SDKs, or transmit personal data without explicit parental gates. You must host a comprehensive Privacy Policy on your registered domain and link it in the console.4.Upload the Bundle and Submit for Review:Release management.Create a new production release, upload your .aab file, complete your store listing metadata (screenshots, descriptions), and submit it for Google's review pipeline.3. Secure the "Teacher Approved" (Apps for Education) BadgeGoogle Play no longer operates a standalone "Google Play for Education" store. Instead, it runs the premium Teacher Approved Program. High-quality apps that pass this evaluation earn a special badge on their public storefront and priority placement in the dedicated "Children" tab.┌──────────────────────────────────────────────┐

How to Get It:You cannot apply directly for the Teacher Approved badge. Instead, inclusion is triggered automatically by Google's review framework:Once you fill out the Target Audience and Content section indicating your app targets children under 13, Google automatically routes the app to a panel of external teachers and child media specialists.The reviewers evaluate your app against strict framework criteria: execution of design, language quality, learning engagement, and developmental appropriateness.If it passes, the Teacher Approved badge is granted automatically to your listing, alongside a breakdown display explaining why teachers liked it.Tip: Your current minimalist layout, hand-drawn aesthetic, and clean math UI are strong baselines for passing this review.4. Get Into Google Play PassGoogle Play Pass is a curated subscription service that offers users hundreds of apps and games completely free of ads and in-app purchases. For developers, it provides an excellent alternative revenue stream based on a programmatic usage metric.Requirements & Application:Ad & IAP Stripping: If your app features ads or monetization, you must integrate the Google Play Billing Architecture so that when a Play Pass subscriber opens the app, all ads are completely blocked and all premium features are unlocked seamlessly. (If your app is already 100% free with no ads, this is trivial).Nomination Basis: Play Pass is primarily an invitation-only program curated by Google. However, Google provides an official expression of interest form for developers.Once your app is live on the Play Store, you can formally submit an application via the Google Play Pass Developer Portal Invitation Interest Form. Google evaluates apps based on engagement metrics, stellar user ratings, cultural alignment, and educational value. Landing the Teacher Approved badge first significantly increases your chances of being fast-tracked into Play Pass.
